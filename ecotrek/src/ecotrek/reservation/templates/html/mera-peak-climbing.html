Mera Peak Climbing - 23 Days

The spectacular Mera Peak (6652m), which is the highest trekking peak of Nepal, lies in the south east of Mt. Everest dominating the remote Hinku and Hunku Valley. The Mera Peak Trekking and climbing takes you to the trail undamaged by the mass trekkers leading to the remote yet beautiful Hinku and Hunku Valley. However, in the recent days the number of people climbing Mera Peak is increasing and is becoming one of the frequently climbed mountains on the list due to its elevation and technical simplicity. It is the challenging journey which requires no previous experience of mountaineering but the physical fitness and sense of adventure. Climbing Mera Peak is an opportunity to observe the world's highest mountains like Everest, Kanchenjunga, Lhotse, Makalu, and Cho Oyo etc, an unparalleled experience of pleasure in the earth. Along with the pleasant climbing of Mera peak, the trek from Lukla to the base camp in an undamaged hill with dense forest is a superb experience.

To reach theMera Base Camp, you begin your journey from Lukla and advance to the east. This trek and climb offers you the wilderness experience. You get the opportunity to explore the wilderness of the Everest region. You pass through the remote and sparsely populated farming villages, dense rhododendron, bamboo and pine forest where you may encounter wild animals like sloth bear and jungle cat if you are lucky along with amazing view of mountains and the valley and finally reach the base camp Mera La. From the base camp it is possible to climb the peak in one day.  From the peak you see the eye-catching view of Kanchanjunga and Makalu in the east, Amadablam and Cho Oyo in the west and towering Mt. Everest, Lhotse and Nuptse in the north and Hinku and Hunku Valley.

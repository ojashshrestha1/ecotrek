Dudh Koshi Rafting

Dudh Koshi River Rafting is one of the major white water rafting in eastern Nepal. Dudh Koshi origins from the region of the highest mountain in the world known as the Everest Region. You can feel the freshness and power of the world highest peak in the white water of Dudh Koshi. Rafting in Dudh Koshi gives you the feeling of being a part of Everest aka Sagarmatha.

Dudh Koshi River Rafting offer rafters with the beautiful scene of Khumbu Himal. Rafting in Dudh Koshi  is divided into two sections as: middle and upper section. The upper section of the river gives rafter a gradient upto 12m/km going deep in the George with a long trail due to the steepness of Dudh Koshi River.

The upper section of Dudh Koshi River Rafting starts in Imju Khola. We arrive middle section of Dudh Koshi River Rafting below the beautiful village of Namche Bazar which is famous as a name, Gateway to the Mountain Everest. During the rafting in Dudh Koshi, rafters can take two types of experience in rafting grades of 3 and 4 rapids.

Day to Day Itinerary

Day 01: Arrival in Kathmandu and transfer to Hotel
Day 02: Sightseeing in Kathmandu Valley.
Day 03: Flight from Kathmandu to Okhaldhunga Airport
Day 04: Trek from Okhaldhunga Airport to Nelabazzar, 5hrs
Day 05: Trek from Nelabazzar to Basa (put in point), 5hrs
Day 06: Rafting from Basa to Demlitar, 5hrs
Day 07: Rafting from Demlitar to Rabuwa, 5hrs
Day 08: Rafting from Rabuwa to Sunkoshi Dovan, 6hrs
Day 09: Rafting from Sunkoshi Dovan to Kuruletar, 5hrs
Day 10: Rafting from Kuruletar to Khuwa Khola, 5hrs
Day 11: Rafting from Khuwa Khola to Chatara (put out point), 3hrs and drive or flight back to Kathmandu.
